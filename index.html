AI Campus Network Hackathon

Project Scoring Sheet

Problem Understanding & Impact
Maximum: 20 Points
How clearly is the problem defined? Is it a relevant and significant real-world challenge? What is the potential positive impact of the solution on its target users or society?
5 - Excellent (20 pts)
Problem is exceptionally clear, highly relevant, and the solution demonstrates profound potential for positive impact.
4 - Good (16 pts)
Problem is clear and relevant, solution has strong potential for positive impact.
3 - Fair (12 pts)
Problem is adequately defined, solution has some potential impact.
2 - Needs Improvement (8 pts)
Problem definition is vague or relevance is questionable, limited potential impact.
1 - Poor (4 pts)
Problem is unclear or irrelevant, no discernible impact.
Innovation & Originality
Maximum: 20 Points
How novel or creative is the proposed solution? Does it offer a unique approach compared to existing methods, or is it a significant improvement on current solutions?
5 - Excellent (20 pts)
Solution is highly innovative and demonstrates significant originality, offering a fresh perspective or breakthrough.
4 - Good (16 pts)
Solution shows strong innovative elements and a creative approach.
3 - Fair (12 pts)
Solution has some innovative aspects but may build heavily on existing ideas.
2 - Needs Improvement (8 pts)
Solution is largely conventional or lacks distinct originality.
1 - Poor (4 pts)
Solution is a direct replication of existing methods with no new ideas.
AI/Technology Implementation
Maximum: 25 Points
How effectively and appropriately is AI/Machine Learning utilized in the solution? Is the technical execution sound? What is the functionality, robustness, and complexity of the prototype developed within the hackathon timeframe?
5 - Excellent (25 pts)
AI/ML is central, highly effective, and technically sophisticated. Prototype is robust, highly functional, and demonstrates complex implementation.
4 - Good (20 pts)
AI/ML is well-integrated and functional. Prototype is mostly functional and demonstrates good technical understanding.
3 - Fair (15 pts)
AI/ML use is present but limited or basic. Prototype has core functionality but may have minor issues.
2 - Needs Improvement (10 pts)
AI/ML use is unclear or ineffective. Prototype has significant functional flaws.
1 - Poor (5 pts)
No discernible AI/ML implementation or prototype is non-functional.
User Experience (UX) & Design (UI)
Maximum: 15 Points
How intuitive, user-friendly, and aesthetically pleasing is the solution's interface? Does the design enhance the user's interaction with the AI functionality?
5 - Excellent (15 pts)
Exceptional UI/UX design; highly intuitive, visually appealing, and enhances user interaction significantly.
4 - Good (12 pts)
Strong UI/UX design; easy to navigate and visually appealing.
3 - Fair (9 pts)
Adequate UI/UX design; functional but may lack polish or optimal user flow.
2 - Needs Improvement (6 pts)
Poor UI/UX design; difficult to navigate or visually unappealing.
1 - Poor (3 pts)
Negligible attention to UI/UX, making the solution unusable.
Feasibility & Scalability
Maximum: 10 Points
Does the solution have realistic potential for future development and implementation? Can it be scaled to reach a larger audience or address a broader scope of the problem?
5 - Excellent (10 pts)
Highly feasible for future development and demonstrates clear, strong scalability potential.
4 - Good (8 pts)
Good feasibility and clear potential for scalability.
3 - Fair (6 pts)
Some feasibility and scalability potential, but may require significant adjustments.
2 - Needs Improvement (4 pts)
Limited feasibility or significant barriers to scaling.
1 - Poor (2 pts)
Not feasible or no apparent path to scalability.
Presentation & Communication
Maximum: 10 Points
How clearly and persuasively was the project presented? Did the team effectively articulate the problem, their solution, its AI components, and its potential impact?
5 - Excellent (10 pts)
Presentation was clear, concise, highly engaging, and effectively conveyed all key aspects of the project.
4 - Good (8 pts)
Presentation was clear, well-structured, and effectively communicated the project's essence.
3 - Fair (6 pts)
Presentation was understandable but may have lacked clarity or engagement in some areas.
2 - Needs Improvement (4 pts)
Presentation was confusing, poorly structured, or failed to convey key information.
1 - Poor (2 pts)
Very poor presentation, making it difficult to understand the project.
